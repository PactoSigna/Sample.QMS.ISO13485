# Production Manager

## Purpose

The Production Manager oversees manufacturing, assembly, and release activities for [Organization Name] products. This role ensures that production processes are validated, controlled, and compliant with ISO 13485 section 7.5 requirements. The Production Manager is responsible for maintaining production infrastructure, managing nonconforming product, and ensuring that finished devices meet all specified requirements before release.

## Responsibilities

- Manage day-to-day production operations, including scheduling, resource allocation, and capacity planning.
- Ensure production processes are validated and maintained under controlled conditions per ISO 13485 section 7.5.2.
- Implement and maintain production environment controls (cleanliness, ESD protection, temperature, humidity) as required by product specifications.
- Manage the Device History Record (DHR) for each production unit or batch, ensuring all required documentation is complete and accurate.
- Oversee incoming inspection of materials and components, ensuring conformance to purchase specifications per [SOP-SUP-001](../sops/SOP-SUP-001.md).
- Execute product release procedures, verifying that all acceptance activities are completed and documented before product is released for distribution.
- Manage nonconforming product per documented procedures, including segregation, disposition, and root cause investigation support.
- Maintain production equipment calibration schedules and records per ISO 13485 section 7.6.
- Coordinate with engineering on design transfer activities, process validation, and production tooling requirements.
- Implement and monitor production quality metrics (yield, scrap, rework rates, cycle times) and drive continuous improvement.
- Ensure proper identification and traceability of product and components throughout the production process per ISO 13485 section 7.5.3.

## Qualifications

- Bachelor's degree in Manufacturing Engineering, Industrial Engineering, Operations Management, or a related field.
- Minimum 5 years of experience in medical device manufacturing or a similarly regulated industry.
- Demonstrated experience with process validation (IQ/OQ/PQ) and production under GMP conditions.
- Knowledge of ISO 13485 production and service provision requirements (section 7.5).
- Experience with lean manufacturing principles and continuous improvement methodologies.
- Familiarity with ERP/MRP systems and production planning tools.
- Strong organizational and people management skills.
- Understanding of cleanroom or controlled environment requirements (if applicable to product type).

## Authorities

- Release finished product for distribution after confirming all DHR requirements are satisfied and acceptance criteria are met.
- Quarantine nonconforming product or materials pending investigation and disposition.
- Halt production activities when quality or safety concerns arise, escalating to the [Quality Manager](./JD-QM-001.md) as needed.
- Approve or reject incoming materials based on inspection results and purchase specifications.
- Authorize rework activities per approved rework procedures.
- Request engineering support for process deviations, tooling changes, or design transfer issues.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to**: VP of Operations or Executive Management.
- **Direct reports**: Production Technicians, Warehouse/Logistics staff, Incoming Inspection personnel.
- **Key collaborators**: [Quality Manager](./JD-QM-001.md) (NCRs, CAPA, process audits), [Software Engineer](./JD-SWE-001.md) (design transfer, production tooling software), [Regulatory Affairs Specialist](./JD-RA-001.md) (labeling, UDI, regulatory requirements for manufacturing).

## Training Requirements

- ISO 13485:2016 production and service provision requirements (section 7.5).
- GMP and clean manufacturing practices (initial + annual refresher).
- Process validation principles (IQ/OQ/PQ) and statistical process control.
- Nonconforming product handling and disposition procedures.
- Calibration management procedures per ISO 13485 section 7.6.
- CAPA process awareness and root cause analysis techniques per [SOP-CAPA-001](../sops/SOP-CAPA-001.md).
- Supplier quality and incoming inspection procedures per [SOP-SUP-001](../sops/SOP-SUP-001.md).
- Device History Record (DHR) completion and review procedures.
- Annual safety training (ESD, chemical handling, ergonomics as applicable).
- Document control procedures per [SOP-DOC-001](../sops/SOP-DOC-001.md).
