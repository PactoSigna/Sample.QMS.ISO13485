# Quality Manager

## Purpose

The Quality Manager serves as the Management Representative per ISO 13485 section 5.5.2. This role owns the effectiveness of the Quality Management System across [Organization Name], ensures regulatory compliance with applicable standards and regulations, and leads management review activities. The Quality Manager has the authority and responsibility to ensure QMS processes are established, implemented, and maintained.

## Responsibilities

- Maintain the Quality Management System in compliance with ISO 13485, FDA 21 CFR Part 820 (QMSR), and applicable regional regulations (EU MDR 2017/745, Health Canada SOR/98-282).
- Oversee document control processes, ensuring all QMS documents are current, reviewed on schedule, and properly approved.
- Manage the CAPA process end-to-end: identification, investigation, root cause analysis oversight, corrective action verification, and effectiveness review per [SOP-CAPA-001](../sops/SOP-CAPA-001.md).
- Coordinate internal audits per the annual audit schedule, assign qualified auditors, and track findings to closure.
- Manage supplier quality, including initial qualification, periodic evaluation, and monitoring of critical suppliers per [SOP-SUP-001](../sops/SOP-SUP-001.md).
- Prepare management review inputs (quality metrics, audit results, CAPA status, customer feedback, complaint trends) and facilitate review meetings per ISO 13485 section 5.6.
- Define training requirements for all roles and ensure training records are complete and current per ISO 13485 section 6.2.
- Oversee the complaint handling process, including intake, investigation, and reportability assessment.
- Ensure post-market surveillance activities are performed and documented.

## Qualifications

- Bachelor's degree in engineering, life sciences, or a related technical field.
- Minimum 5 years of experience in quality management within the medical device industry.
- Demonstrated experience with ISO 13485 implementation and maintenance.
- ISO 13485 Lead Auditor certification preferred.
- Experience with electronic QMS platforms and document management systems.
- Strong understanding of FDA regulatory requirements, including 21 CFR Part 820 and 21 CFR Part 11.
- Knowledge of risk management principles per ISO 14971.
- Effective communication and leadership skills for cross-functional collaboration.

## Authorities

- Approve or reject QMS documents (SOPs, policies, work instructions, job descriptions) as final approver for the Quality department.
- Initiate CAPAs, nonconformance reports, and complaint investigations.
- Halt production or release activities for quality concerns by withholding quality approval.
- Define and approve access control policies for QMS documentation.
- Escalate quality or safety concerns directly to executive management.
- Approve or reject supplier qualifications and audit results.
- Define training requirements and approve training completion records.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to**: CEO or Executive Management.
- **Direct reports**: QA Engineers, Document Control Specialists.
- **Key collaborators**: [Regulatory Affairs Specialist](./JD-RA-001.md) (compliance, audit findings), [Software Engineer](./JD-SWE-001.md) (design control, code reviews), [Production Manager](./JD-PROD-001.md) (manufacturing quality, NCRs).

## Training Requirements

- ISO 13485:2016 internal auditor training (initial + triennial refresher).
- FDA 21 CFR Part 11 electronic records and signatures.
- ISO 14971 risk management for medical devices.
- IEC 62304 software lifecycle fundamentals (if applicable to product type).
- CAPA management and root cause analysis techniques per [SOP-CAPA-001](../sops/SOP-CAPA-001.md).
- Complaint handling and adverse event reporting procedures.
- Supplier quality management per [SOP-SUP-001](../sops/SOP-SUP-001.md).
- Annual GMP/QMS refresher training.
- Document control and records management procedures per [SOP-DOC-001](../sops/SOP-DOC-001.md).
