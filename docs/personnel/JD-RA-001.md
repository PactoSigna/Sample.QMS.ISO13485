# Regulatory Affairs Specialist

## Purpose

The Regulatory Affairs Specialist ensures that [Organization Name] products meet regulatory requirements across target markets and supports regulatory submissions. This role is responsible for maintaining awareness of current regulations and standards, performing compliance gap analysis, and ensuring that product documentation aligns with regulatory expectations for the applicable device classification.

## Responsibilities

- Monitor the regulatory landscape for changes to standards (ISO 13485, IEC 62304, ISO 14971), regulations (FDA QMSR, EU MDR 2017/745), and guidance documents that affect [Organization Name] products.
- Prepare and maintain regulatory submission documentation, including 510(k) submissions, CE Technical Files, and Declaration of Conformity packages.
- Perform compliance gap analysis between product documentation and applicable regulatory requirements.
- Support design control activities by reviewing intended use statements, clinical evaluation documentation, and labeling for regulatory alignment.
- Coordinate post-market surveillance activities, including feedback collection, trend analysis, and reportability assessment per [SOP-PMS-001](../sops/SOP-PMS-001.md).
- Provide device classification guidance based on applicable frameworks (IMDRF, FDA, EU MDR).
- Review and approve regulatory document content (intended use, clinical evaluation plans and reports, labeling, IFUs).
- Support internal and external audits by preparing regulatory compliance evidence and responding to auditor queries.
- Communicate regulatory impacts of proposed product changes to engineering and quality teams.

## Qualifications

- Bachelor's or Master's degree in Regulatory Affairs, Biomedical Engineering, Life Sciences, or a related field.
- Minimum 4 years of regulatory affairs experience with medical device products.
- Demonstrated experience with FDA 510(k) or De Novo submissions.
- Knowledge of EU MDR 2017/745 classification and conformity assessment procedures.
- RAC (Regulatory Affairs Certification) or equivalent professional certification preferred.
- Familiarity with IMDRF guidance documents and harmonized standards.
- Strong analytical and technical writing skills.
- Experience with electronic QMS and document management systems.

## Authorities

- Flag regulatory non-compliance issues that may block product release, requiring resolution before approval can proceed.
- Approve regulatory document content as final approver for the Regulatory department (intended use, clinical evaluation, labeling documents).
- Escalate safety concerns or potential adverse events to the [Quality Manager](./JD-QM-001.md) and executive management.
- Initiate regulatory impact assessments for proposed product or process changes.
- Determine reportability of complaints and adverse events to regulatory authorities.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to**: [Quality Manager](./JD-QM-001.md) or Executive Management.
- **Key collaborators**: [Quality Manager](./JD-QM-001.md) (CAPA, audit findings, complaint handling), [Software Engineer](./JD-SWE-001.md) (design control documentation, software lifecycle), [Production Manager](./JD-PROD-001.md) (labeling, manufacturing documentation).

## Training Requirements

- ISO 13485:2016 quality management systems for medical devices.
- EU MDR 2017/745 classification and conformity assessment procedures.
- FDA 21 CFR Part 820 (QMSR) quality system regulation.
- FDA 21 CFR Part 11 electronic records and signatures awareness.
- IEC 62304 regulatory requirements for software lifecycle processes (if applicable to product type).
- ISO 14971 risk management for medical devices.
- Post-market surveillance procedures per [SOP-PMS-001](../sops/SOP-PMS-001.md).
- Complaint handling and adverse event reporting procedures.
- Health Canada Medical Devices Regulations (SOR/98-282) and MDSAP audit methodology (if applicable to target markets).
- Annual regulatory intelligence update (standards revisions, enforcement actions, guidance publications).
