# SOP-006: Management Review

## 1. Purpose

Ensure the continued suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the Quality Management System through planned management reviews at defined intervals.

## 2. Scope

Applies to the review of the entire QMS, including quality policy, quality objectives, process performance, and regulatory compliance. Includes all products within the QMS scope.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Management Review | Formal evaluation of the QMS by top management |
| Quality Management Representative | Person responsible for preparing review inputs and tracking outputs |

## 4. Responsibilities

- **Top Management**: Attend and conduct management reviews, make resource and improvement decisions
- **Quality Management Representative**: Schedule reviews, prepare input data, distribute agenda, record minutes, track action items
- **Department Heads**: Provide process performance data for their areas

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Frequency

Management reviews are conducted:

- At least annually (scheduled in Q1)
- After significant events: major nonconformances, regulatory findings, product recalls, organizational changes

### 5.2 Attendees

Required attendees:

- CEO or designee
- Quality Management Representative
- Department heads (Engineering, Quality, Regulatory)

Optional attendees:

- Clinical, Security, Operations leads as needed based on agenda

### 5.3 Review Inputs

The Quality Management Representative prepares the following data:

1. Status of actions from previous management reviews
2. Internal and external audit results
3. Customer feedback and complaint trends
4. Process performance and product conformity metrics
5. CAPA status and effectiveness
6. Changes that could affect the QMS (regulatory, organizational, technological)
7. Supplier performance summary
8. Training compliance status
9. Post-market surveillance data
10. Recommendations for improvement
11. New or revised regulatory requirements

### 5.4 Conducting the Review

1. Quality Management Representative distributes agenda and input data at least 5 business days before the meeting
2. Each input area is reviewed and discussed
3. Decisions are recorded in meeting minutes
4. Action items are assigned with owners and due dates

### 5.5 Review Outputs

Management review outputs include decisions and actions related to:

- Improvement of the QMS and its processes
- Improvement of product related to customer and regulatory requirements
- Resource needs (personnel, infrastructure, training)
- Changes to quality policy or quality objectives (if warranted)

### 5.6 Follow-up

1. Meeting minutes distributed within 5 business days
2. Action items tracked to completion
3. Status reviewed at next management review

## 6. Records

- Management review agenda
- Management review minutes (including decisions and action items)
- Action item tracking log
