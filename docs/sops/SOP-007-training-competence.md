# SOP-007: Training and Competence

## 1. Purpose

Ensure all personnel performing work affecting product quality are competent on the basis of appropriate education, training, skills, and experience.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all personnel (employees, contractors, consultants) who perform activities within the QMS scope, including design, development, testing, quality assurance, regulatory affairs, and management.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Competency | Demonstrated ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results |
| Training Plan | Documented plan identifying required training for a role or individual |
| Training Effectiveness | Evaluation of whether training achieved its intended learning objectives |

## 4. Responsibilities

- **Quality**: Maintain the competency matrix, administer the training program, track training compliance
- **Department Heads**: Define competency requirements for their roles, identify training needs, ensure personnel complete required training
- **All Personnel**: Complete assigned training within due dates, apply learned skills

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Competency Requirements

Each role has defined competency requirements documented in the competency matrix:

| Competency Area | Example Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| QMS Fundamentals | ISO 13485, document control, CAPA |
| Design Controls | IEC 62304, software lifecycle, risk management |
| Regulatory | Applicable regulations (MDR, FDA), submission processes |
| Role-Specific | Technical skills, tools, domain knowledge |

### 5.2 Training Needs Identification

Training needs are identified through:

- New hire onboarding
- Role changes or promotions
- New or updated SOPs and work instructions
- Nonconformances or CAPA findings indicating knowledge gaps
- New regulatory requirements
- Annual competency review

### 5.3 Training Delivery Methods

| Method | When Used | Verification |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Quiz-based | SOPs with testable knowledge | Pass quiz with ≥ 80% score |
| Acknowledgment | Policies and informational documents | Electronic acknowledgment recorded |
| Instructor-led | Hands-on skills, complex procedures | Instructor sign-off |
| On-the-job | Practical skills application | Supervisor verification |

### 5.4 New Employee Onboarding

New employees complete the following within 30 days:

1. Quality Policy acknowledgment (POL-001)
2. Quality Manual acknowledgment (QM-001)
3. Document Control training (SOP-001)
4. Role-specific SOP training as identified by department head

### 5.5 SOP Update Re-training

When an SOP is updated:

1. The QMS platform automatically assigns re-training to personnel in the SOP's required departments
2. Personnel complete training within the specified due period
3. Quality monitors compliance and escalates overdue training

### 5.6 Training Effectiveness Evaluation

Training effectiveness is evaluated through:

- Quiz scores and completion rates
- Observation of on-the-job performance
- Reduction in errors or nonconformances related to the trained area
- Annual competency review

### 5.7 Training Records

For each training event, the following is recorded:

- Trainee name and role
- Training topic and associated document
- Date of completion
- Training method
- Result (pass/fail, score, acknowledgment)
- Trainer name (for instructor-led training)

## 6. Records

- Competency matrix
- Individual training records
- Training effectiveness evaluations
- Training compliance reports
