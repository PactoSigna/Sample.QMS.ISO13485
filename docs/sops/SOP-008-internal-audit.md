# SOP-008: Internal Audit

## 1. Purpose

Plan and conduct internal audits to verify the effectiveness of the QMS and ensure compliance with ISO 13485:2016, applicable regulatory requirements, and organizational procedures.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all processes, departments, and activities within the QMS scope. All ISO 13485 clauses must be audited at least once within the audit cycle.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Audit Program | Schedule of audits covering the full QMS over a defined cycle |
| Lead Auditor | Person responsible for planning and conducting a specific audit |
| Auditee | Person or department being audited |
| Finding | Result of evaluating audit evidence against audit criteria |
| Major Finding | Absence or total breakdown of a required system element |
| Minor Finding | Isolated lapse in compliance that does not represent a systemic failure |
| Observation | Opportunity for improvement that does not constitute a nonconformance |

## 4. Responsibilities

- **Quality Management Representative**: Establish and maintain the audit program, assign auditors, track findings to closure
- **Lead Auditor**: Plan and conduct audits, report findings, verify corrective action effectiveness
- **Auditees**: Cooperate with auditors, provide access to records and processes, implement corrective actions
- **Top Management**: Review audit results during management review, provide resources

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Audit Program

1. Quality establishes an annual audit program covering all QMS processes and ISO 13485 clauses
2. Audit frequency is based on process risk and previous findings:
   - High risk or previous nonconformances: at least annually
   - Standard risk: at least every 2 years within the full cycle
3. The audit program is approved by the Quality Management Representative

### 5.2 Auditor Qualification

- Auditors must complete internal audit training (ISO 19011 principles)
- Auditors must be independent of the area being audited
- Lead auditors must have conducted at least 2 audits under supervision

### 5.3 Audit Planning

For each audit:

1. Lead auditor prepares an audit plan including scope, criteria, schedule, and methods
2. Audit plan distributed to auditee at least 5 business days before the audit
3. Lead auditor reviews relevant documentation before the audit

### 5.4 Audit Execution

1. **Opening meeting**: Confirm scope, schedule, and logistics with auditee
2. **Evidence collection**: Review records, observe processes, interview personnel
3. **Finding classification**: Classify observations as major finding, minor finding, or observation
4. **Closing meeting**: Present findings to auditee, agree on factual accuracy

### 5.5 Audit Reporting

1. Lead auditor prepares audit report within 10 business days
2. Report includes: scope, criteria, findings (classified), observations, auditor conclusion
3. Report distributed to auditee and Quality Management Representative

### 5.6 Follow-up

1. Major and minor findings require CAPA (per [SOP-004](SOP-004-capa.md))
2. Auditee proposes corrective actions within 15 business days
3. Lead auditor verifies corrective action effectiveness
4. Findings tracked to closure in the finding log
5. Audit results are input to management review (per [SOP-006](SOP-006-management-review.md))

## 6. Records

- Annual audit program
- Audit plans
- Audit reports
- Finding log
- Corrective action verification records
